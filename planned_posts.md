# Planned Blog Posts

## Pain-point / Problem Awareness

- **The Hidden Cost of Process Debt: When Your Team Works Off Bad Docs**
  The financial and operational cost of teams running on outdated processes — slow onboarding, inconsistent execution, compliance exposure.

- **Google Docs Is Not a Process System**
  Why wiki-style tools (Notion, Confluence, Google Drive) fall short for operational documentation — and what a real process system needs to do differently.

- **The 5 Signs Your Team Has Outgrown Notion for Operations**
  A listicle for teams at the 20–100 person inflection point: specific symptoms that signal informal ops tools are holding them back.

## Thought Leadership / Positioning

- **What "Living Documentation" Actually Means (and Why Static SOPs Fail)**
  A definitional piece: distinguishing living docs from static docs, and what properties make a process document genuinely maintainable.

- **Who Owns This Process? The Ownership Problem in Team Operations**
  Explores why "everyone's responsible" means nobody is — and what explicit process ownership looks like in practice.

- **SLAs Are Promises — Are Your Processes Built to Keep Them?**
  Connects the gap between SLA commitments and the operational processes that actually deliver (or fail to deliver) on them.

## Practical / How-to (SEO-Friendly)

- **How to Write an SOP That Doesn't Get Ignored**
  Practical guide to SOP structure, length, format, and the ownership decisions that make the difference between a doc people use and one that gathers dust.

- **Building a Process Review Cadence That Teams Actually Follow**
  How to design a review system that survives the chaos of a busy team — triggers, owners, frequency, and lightweight formats.

- **How to Audit Your Team's Operational Knowledge Before It Walks Out the Door**
  A practical framework for identifying and capturing undocumented institutional knowledge before key people leave.

- **The Difference Between a Checklist, a Runbook, and a Process Spec**
  Clears up a common source of confusion — when to use each format and how they relate to each other.

## Audience-Specific

- **Operations at 10 vs. 50 vs. 100 People: What Changes and When**
  A stage-by-stage breakdown of how operational needs evolve and what to prioritize at each threshold.

- **What Ops Managers Wish Founders Understood About Process Documentation**
  Framed from the ops manager's perspective — common founder assumptions that create operational debt downstream.
